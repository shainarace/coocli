COOCLI Survey Analysis

2 Other Rurality

2.1 Conclusions from this Analysis

  • As rurality increases, the proportion of respondents reporting increased condom usage since participating in the exchange decreases.
  • As rurality increases, it appears the strain on NCHRC staff also grows.
  • Urban respondents have 25% higher odds of reporting barriers in their attempt to access treatment than their rural counterparts.

2.4 Barriers to Treatment

When it comes to participants who sought treatment but were unable to access it for any reason, we were surprised to learn that such experiences were generally more likely to happen in urban areas than in rural areas. This result shows statistical significance in this dataset, although we didn’t see the same granularity of the relationship with the levels of rurality that we saw in previous charts.

Figure 2.3: A one unit increase in rural score corresponds to 20% reduction in the odds of reporting an inability to access desired treatment.
