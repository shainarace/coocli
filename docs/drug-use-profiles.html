COOCLI Survey Analysis

4 Drug-use profiles

4.1 Conclusions from this analysis

  • A one unit increase in rural score corresponds to nearly a doubling of the odds of using meth and presciption stimulants.
  • A one unit increase in rural score corresponds to an expected 33% reduction in the odds of using opioids other than those associated with Medication Assisted Treatment (MAT).
  • A one unit increase in rural score corresponds to an expected 45% reduction in the odds of using cocaine.
  • Those on Medication Assisted Treatments are about 64% times more likely to express interest in services like counseling or support groups
  • This is a robust result that doesn’t appear to be located in any one location or demographic group

4.2 Analysis

We lump substances into 5 groups, separating cocaine stimulants (powder, crack, speedball) from amphetamine stimulants (meth, adderall). We also separate MAT opioids from the other opioids. These groups create binary variables, giving no weight to those using multiple substances within a category. We have a fairly representative distribution of participants across the 5 groups, which are not mutually exclusive (someone can use MAT and stimulants for instance).

4.2.2 Services of Interest

In looking for differences among services of interest, only one notable finding jumped out. Those on MAT are more likely to express interest in services like counseling or support groups. This could simply reflect a higher situation on Maslow’s hierarchy of needs, but it stands as evidence that drug users don’t simply use MAT as a “crutch” but rather as a starting place on a long journey towards a holistic recovery.

Figure 4.4: Those on Medication Assisted Treatments are about 64% times more likely to express interest in services like counseling or support groups.

The sample size for MAT users in the data is large, and the difference on these questions is statistically notable.
